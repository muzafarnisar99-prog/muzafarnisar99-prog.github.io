Permanent Depigmentation for Vitiligo — Complete Guide

📅 April 2026 · monobenzone.shop · Medical Information

What is Permanent Depigmentation for Vitiligo?

Permanent depigmentation is a therapeutic approach for severe vitiligo where the remaining pigmented skin is permanently lightened to match the already-depigmented vitiligo patches — creating a uniform skin appearance.

This approach is considered when vitiligo affects more than 50% of the body surface area and repigmentation therapy has been unsuccessful or is not feasible.

Is Permanent Depigmentation Right for You?

Depigmentation therapy may be appropriate if:

  • Vitiligo affects more than 50% of your body surface
  • Multiple repigmentation treatments have failed (steroids, tacrolimus, NB-UVB, PUVA)
  • The psychological burden of vitiligo is significantly impacting quality of life
  • You have made an informed decision after consulting a dermatologist

The Treatment Process

Step 1: Dermatologist consultation and assessment of vitiligo extent

Step 2: Starting with monobenzone 20% cream applied twice daily to pigmented areas

Step 3: Regular monitoring — results begin in 1-4 months

Step 4: Treatment intensification if needed (increasing to 40% or twice daily)

Step 5: Maintenance and lifelong sun protection

✅ Clinical evidence from PMC3533321 shows excellent results with monobenzone 20% cream, with results confirmed stable at 3-year follow-up and significant quality of life improvement.

Psychosocial Benefits

Research shows vitiligo patients experience a rate of depression twice that of the general population. Successful depigmentation therapy has been shown to significantly improve quality of life, self-confidence, and psychological wellbeing in vitiligo patients.
