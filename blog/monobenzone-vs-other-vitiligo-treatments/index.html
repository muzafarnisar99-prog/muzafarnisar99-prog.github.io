With so many vitiligo treatment options available, patients often ask: which treatment is actually best? This comprehensive comparison covers monobenzone vs other vitiligo treatments — helping you understand when each is appropriate, what the evidence shows, and which makes practical sense for your situation.

When is Depigmentation Preferred to Repigmentation?

Vitiligo treatment falls into two broad categories:

  • Repigmentation — restoring color to white patches (steroids, tacrolimus, NB-UVB, PUVA, excimer laser)
  • Depigmentation — removing remaining pigment for uniform appearance (monobenzone)

According to dermatological guidelines, depigmentation with monobenzone is preferred when:

  • Vitiligo affects more than 50% of body surface area
  • Multiple repigmentation treatments have failed over 12+ months
  • The patient prioritizes uniform appearance over restoring original skin color
  • Vitiligo is rapidly progressive with no stable response to repigmentation
✅ Monobenzone is the only FDA-approved treatment for permanent depigmentation in extensive vitiligo — with a safety record since 1952.

Monobenzone vs Hydroquinone vs Natural Oils — Pros & Cons

Monobenzone (MBEH) 20% / 40%

  • ✅ Most potent depigmenting agent available
  • ✅ FDA-approved since 1952
  • ✅ Permanent results — no ongoing treatment needed
  • ✅ Clinically proven — results documented in peer-reviewed studies
  • ❌ Permanent and irreversible — cannot be undone
  • ❌ Requires strict sun protection for life
  • ❌ May cause skin irritation initially

Hydroquinone (2–4%)

  • ✅ Available in many countries without prescription
  • ✅ Useful for cosmetic hyperpigmentation (not vitiligo-specific)
  • ❌ Temporary — results reverse when stopped
  • ❌ Not FDA-approved for vitiligo depigmentation
  • ❌ Much weaker than monobenzone for extensive vitiligo
  • ❌ Banned in some countries due to safety concerns at high concentrations

Natural Oils (Bergamot, Black seed, etc.)

  • ✅ Widely available and inexpensive
  • ❌ No clinical evidence for vitiligo depigmentation
  • ❌ Some (bergamot) can cause photosensitivity burns
  • ❌ Not recommended by dermatologists for extensive vitiligo
⚕️ Only monobenzone is FDA-approved for permanent vitiligo depigmentation. Natural oils and cosmetic bleaches are not appropriate for extensive vitiligo treatment.
