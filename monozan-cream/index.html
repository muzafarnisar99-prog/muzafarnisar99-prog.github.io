What is Monozan Cream?

Monozan (also spelled Monozan) is a brand name for monobenzone topical cream manufactured by Behvazan Pharmaceutical Co. in Rasht, Iran. Monozan cream contains monobenzone (monobenzyl ether of hydroquinone / MBEH) as the active pharmaceutical ingredient — the same active ingredient found in the well-known Benoquin cream.

Monozan cream is used exclusively for permanent depigmentation in vitiligo patients. It is available in 20% concentration (standard strength) and 40% concentration (higher strength) — making monozan the most versatile monobenzone product available on the market today.

Monozan Skin Depigmentation Cream — How it Works

Monozan monobenzone cream achieves permanent skin depigmentation through a well-established mechanism:

  1. Free-radical cytotoxicity — destroys melanocytes (pigment cells) permanently via oxidative stress
  2. Tyrosinase inhibition — blocks the key enzyme in melanin production

The combined effect is gradual, permanent lightening of treated skin to match the depigmented vitiligo patches. Monozan/monobenzone is the most potent depigmenting agent available — the best depigmentation cream for full body vitiligo patients.

Monozan Cream — Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between Monozan and Monozan?
Monozan and Monozan are alternate spellings/transliterations of the same product — Behvazan Pharmaceutical Co.'s monobenzone cream. Both refer to the same pharmaceutical product containing monobenzone 20% as the active ingredient.
Is Monozan cream the same as Benoquin?
Yes — both Monozan and Benoquin contain monobenzone as the active ingredient. Benoquin was an ICN Pharmaceuticals brand (now discontinued) while Monozan is manufactured by Behvazan Pharmaceutical Co. The therapeutic effect is identical.
What is Monozan cream used for?
Monozan cream is used for permanent depigmentation in patients with extensive vitiligo — particularly when vitiligo affects more than 50% of body surface and repigmentation therapy has been unsuccessful.
How long does Monozan cream take to work?
Results typically begin in 1–4 months of twice-daily application. Complete depigmentation of targeted areas is usually achieved within 9–12 months. A clinical study confirmed excellent results within 3.5 months with stability at 3-year follow-up.
