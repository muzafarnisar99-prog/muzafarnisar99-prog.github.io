What is Benoquin Cream?

Benoquin cream is a well-known brand name for monobenzone 20% topical cream, formerly manufactured by ICN Pharmaceuticals (later Valeant Pharmaceuticals). Benoquin was FDA-approved since 1952 for the permanent depigmentation of extensive vitiligo — one of the longest-standing pharmaceutical approvals in dermatology.

The active ingredient in Benoquin cream is monobenzone (monobenzyl ether of hydroquinone / MBEH) — a powerful depigmenting agent that permanently destroys melanocytes in treated skin areas, achieving a uniform skin tone for vitiligo patients.

How Benoquin / Monobenzone Works

Monobenzone (Benoquin active ingredient) achieves permanent depigmentation through two mechanisms:

  1. Free-radical formation — permanently destroys melanocytes (pigment-producing cells)
  2. Tyrosinase inhibition — blocks the enzyme responsible for melanin production

The result is gradual, permanent lightening of treated skin to match vitiligo-depigmented areas. This is why Benoquin/monobenzone is the treatment of choice for patients with extensive vitiligo covering more than 50% of body surface.

⚕️ Monobenzone (Benoquin) causes permanent and irreversible depigmentation. Use strictly for vitiligo under dermatological supervision. Not for cosmetic skin lightening.
